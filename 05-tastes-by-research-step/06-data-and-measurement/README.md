# Step: Data and Measurement

Build or choose measures that actually capture the concept.

## What Good Taste Looks Like

- The step advances the paper's core contribution.
- The choices are justified by the research question, not convenience.
- The strongest skeptical objection is anticipated.
- The next step becomes clearer after completing this one.

## What Bad Taste Looks Like

- The step is performed mechanically.
- The paper adds complexity without learning.
- The author avoids the hardest objection.
- The section looks polished but does not improve the argument.

## Key Questions

1. What decision is being made at this step?
2. What would a top scholar ask here?
3. What would a skeptical referee doubt?
4. What evidence or argument would make this stronger?
5. What should be recorded in the taste log?
