# Good Identification Taste

Good identification makes a skeptical reader believe the comparison is informative.

## What Good Taste Looks Like

- The source of variation is clear.
- Threats are named directly.
- The design has transparent assumptions.
- Falsification and placebo tests teach something.

## What Bad Taste Looks Like

- The method is treated as credibility by itself.
- The comparison group is convenient but not convincing.
- Robustness checks are mechanical.
- The design cannot separate mechanisms.

## Economics Lens

Ask whether the idea improves how we understand behavior, policy, markets, institutions, welfare, or causal mechanisms.

## Finance Lens

Ask whether the idea improves how we understand prices, risks, beliefs, frictions, constraints, intermediaries, firms, or investors.

## Practice Exercise

Pick one current research idea and diagnose this dimension. Write one paragraph on what is strong, one paragraph on what is weak, and one concrete test or revision.
