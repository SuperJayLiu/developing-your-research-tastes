# Good Theory Taste

A good theory isolates a friction and generates predictions that could be wrong.

## What Good Taste Looks Like

- It names the agents, incentives, constraints, and mechanism.
- It separates itself from alternative explanations.
- It produces testable predictions.
- It explains why the effect matters economically.

## What Bad Taste Looks Like

- It explains everything after the fact.
- It uses vague mechanisms.
- It has no falsifiable prediction.
- It is too complex to clarify judgment.

## Economics Lens

Ask whether the idea improves how we understand behavior, policy, markets, institutions, welfare, or causal mechanisms.

## Finance Lens

Ask whether the idea improves how we understand prices, risks, beliefs, frictions, constraints, intermediaries, firms, or investors.

## Practice Exercise

Pick one current research idea and diagnose this dimension. Write one paragraph on what is strong, one paragraph on what is weak, and one concrete test or revision.
