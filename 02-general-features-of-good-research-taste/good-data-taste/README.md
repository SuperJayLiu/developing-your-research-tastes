# Good Data Taste

Good data taste means understanding what the data can and cannot say.

## What Good Taste Looks Like

- The data are appropriate for the concept.
- Construction choices are transparent.
- Missingness and selection are discussed.
- The data open a question that was previously hard to answer.

## What Bad Taste Looks Like

- The dataset is large but not conceptually matched.
- Cleaning decisions are invisible.
- Data availability drives the question too much.
- The paper overclaims from limited coverage.

## Economics Lens

Ask whether the idea improves how we understand behavior, policy, markets, institutions, welfare, or causal mechanisms.

## Finance Lens

Ask whether the idea improves how we understand prices, risks, beliefs, frictions, constraints, intermediaries, firms, or investors.

## Practice Exercise

Pick one current research idea and diagnose this dimension. Write one paragraph on what is strong, one paragraph on what is weak, and one concrete test or revision.
