# Define factors by economic sorts, not black boxes

## Source Scholar

Kenneth French

## Generalisable Skill

Construct factors from characteristics with interpretable economic meaning rather than purely
statistical extraction.

## Evidence Anchors

- Three-factor model
- Five-factor model
- Size and value portfolio papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests examine whether portfolios based on size, value, profitability, or investment explain
returns.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The writing connects each factor to an economic candidate mechanism while remaining empirical.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by making each latent factor explainable in one economic sentence.

### Critical boundary

The risk is that interpretable labels may hide multiple mechanisms.

## Practice Exercise

Take one factor and list at least two theories that could generate it.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
