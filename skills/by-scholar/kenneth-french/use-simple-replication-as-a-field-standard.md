# Use simple replication as a field standard

## Source Scholar

Kenneth French

## Generalisable Skill

Make empirical design simple enough that anomalies and models can be replicated and compared.

## Evidence Anchors

- French Data Library
- Factor model papers
- Portfolio sort papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests use standard definitions and public data to lower the cost of scrutiny.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction frames contribution through comparability and breadth rather than novelty alone.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill by choosing definitions that others can reproduce easily unless complexity is
essential.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is that standardization can suppress context-specific mechanisms.

## Practice Exercise

Rewrite a complex empirical design into a minimal replicable version.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
