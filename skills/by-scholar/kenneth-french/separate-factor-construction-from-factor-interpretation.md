# Separate factor construction from factor interpretation

## Source Scholar

Kenneth French

## Generalisable Skill

Distinguish the mechanical return factor from the theory that explains why it earns a premium.

## Evidence Anchors

- Common Risk Factors
- Five-Factor Model
- Cross-sectional returns papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests can show explanatory power without fully settling risk versus mispricing interpretation.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The writing often presents the factor first as an empirical object, then discusses interpretation.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by naming which part of your result is construction and which part is explanation.

### Critical boundary

The critical boundary is overinterpretation: a factor is not automatically a structural mechanism.

## Practice Exercise

Write two paragraphs for one factor: how it is built and what it might mean.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
