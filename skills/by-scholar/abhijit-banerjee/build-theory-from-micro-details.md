# Build theory from micro details

## Source Scholar

Abhijit Banerjee

## Generalisable Skill

Use granular observations about households, firms, or institutions to discipline which model is
worth writing.

## Evidence Anchors

- The Economic Lives of the Poor
- Poor Economics
- Remedying Education

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests often begin with behavior that standard models do not immediately explain, then test
constraints, beliefs, or frictions.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The writing style frequently starts with a concrete human or institutional puzzle before
generalizing.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by collecting descriptive facts before choosing the theoretical frame.

### Critical boundary

The risk is anecdotal theorizing. The detail must generate testable mechanisms, not just colorful
motivation.

## Practice Exercise

Take one descriptive fact and write two competing models that could explain it.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
