# Write accessible introductions with analytical bite

## Source Scholar

Abhijit Banerjee

## Generalisable Skill

Make a paper readable by beginning with a concrete question, but preserve rigor by quickly
identifying the mechanism and evidence.

## Evidence Anchors

- The Economic Lives of the Poor
- Poor Economics-related papers
- Field experiment papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The introduction/abstract style often avoids unnecessary jargon while still moving toward a testable
claim.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The empirical work then supports the accessible story with design, measurement, and mechanisms.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill by writing the first paragraph for an intelligent non-specialist and the second for a
skeptical economist.

### Critical boundary

The danger is popular clarity without identification clarity. The introduction must not substitute
vividness for evidence.

## Practice Exercise

Write two opening paragraphs: one motivating the puzzle, one stating the research design.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
