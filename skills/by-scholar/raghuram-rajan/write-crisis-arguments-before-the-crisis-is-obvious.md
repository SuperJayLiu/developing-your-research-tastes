# Write crisis arguments before the crisis is obvious

## Source Scholar

Raghuram Rajan

## Generalisable Skill

Notice fragile incentives and systemic risks before they become consensus.

## Evidence Anchors

- Has Financial Development Made the World Riskier?
- Banking and liquidity papers
- Crisis-related writing

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests and theory examine incentives, risk-taking, and intermediation structures that can amplify
shocks.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction frames innovation and development as potentially stability-reducing, not
automatically good.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by asking how a beneficial financial innovation could create hidden tail risk.

### Critical boundary

The risk is alarmism. Good taste identifies the mechanism and conditions for fragility.

## Practice Exercise

Take one financial innovation and write both its efficiency benefit and fragility channel.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
