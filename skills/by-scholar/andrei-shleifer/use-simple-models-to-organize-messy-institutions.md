# Use simple models to organize messy institutions

## Source Scholar

Andrei Shleifer

## Generalisable Skill

Reduce legal, governance, or political complexity to a core economic friction while preserving
institutional relevance.

## Evidence Anchors

- Law and Finance
- Corporate governance survey
- Transition-economy papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests often use cross-country legal variation, ownership structures, or governance mechanisms.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction names a simple institutional channel that can explain broad outcomes.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by finding the one contractual or legal constraint that drives the setting.

### Critical boundary

The risk is institutional oversimplification. A good paper knows which details matter and which can
be abstracted.

## Practice Exercise

Write the core friction in one sentence and three institutional details it abstracts from.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
