# Write bold claims with a clear friction

## Source Scholar

Andrei Shleifer

## Generalisable Skill

Make ambitious arguments credible by centering them on a specific mechanism.

## Evidence Anchors

- Law and Finance
- Limits of Arbitrage
- Investor sentiment papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The empirical and theoretical work often supports large claims through a simple organizing friction.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction style is direct: a field-level puzzle, a friction, and evidence across settings.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by removing secondary mechanisms until the core claim is visible.

### Critical boundary

The risk is excessive breadth. Boldness must be matched with scope conditions.

## Practice Exercise

Write your paper's boldest claim and the single friction that supports it.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
