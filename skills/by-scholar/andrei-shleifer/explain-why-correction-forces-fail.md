# Explain why correction forces fail

## Source Scholar

Andrei Shleifer

## Generalisable Skill

When markets or institutions produce bad outcomes, identify why competition, arbitrage, entry, or
governance fails to fix them.

## Evidence Anchors

- Limits of Arbitrage
- Corporate governance studies
- Law and finance work

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests look for barriers to correction: risk, coordination, law, agency, information, or
incentives.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction often moves from 'this should not persist' to 'here is why it persists.'

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill in markets, firms, politics, and organizations.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is that every failure story needs a counterforce. Good taste quantifies or documents
why it is weak.

## Practice Exercise

For any persistent inefficiency, list the correction force and why it is blocked.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
