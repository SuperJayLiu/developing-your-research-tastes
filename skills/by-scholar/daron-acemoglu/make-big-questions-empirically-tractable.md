# Make big questions empirically tractable

## Source Scholar

Daron Acemoglu

## Generalisable Skill

Start from a civilization-scale or economy-wide question, but reduce it to a proxy, treatment, model
object, or comparison that can be tested.

## Evidence Anchors

- The Colonial Origins of Comparative Development
- Robots and Jobs
- The Race between Man and Machine

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests typically convert broad ideas such as institutions, technology, democracy, or automation
into measurable exposure variables and then ask whether predicted comparative statics appear in
data.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The writing move is to keep the ambition high while quickly telling the reader what the paper can
actually observe, estimate, or model.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by forcing every broad research question to pass through a tractability filter: what is
the observable variation, what is the unit of analysis, and what prediction could fail?

### Critical boundary

The danger is pseudo-tractability: a narrow proxy can be mistaken for the whole concept. The
introduction should say what the proxy captures and what it cannot capture.

## Practice Exercise

Rewrite one broad idea in your field into three empirically testable versions, each with a different
unit of analysis.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
