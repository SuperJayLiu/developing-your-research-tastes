# Combine theory with historical or institutional evidence

## Source Scholar

Daron Acemoglu

## Generalisable Skill

Use a model to discipline the mechanism, then use historical or institutional variation to show the
mechanism has empirical bite.

## Evidence Anchors

- Reversal of Fortune
- Why Nations Fail
- Directed Technical Change

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

Theory supplies the mechanism and comparative statics; historical or empirical evidence checks
whether the mechanism helps explain observed variation rather than remaining a verbal story.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction often works by first naming a puzzle, then identifying a mechanism, then promising
evidence that the mechanism is not just plausible but consequential.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill when a story feels persuasive but loose. Write the mechanism as a chain of incentives
and then ask what empirical pattern the chain predicts.

### Critical boundary

The risk is confirmation bias: theory can make a preferred historical narrative sound inevitable. A
strong page should list rival mechanisms and evidence that would weaken the claim.

## Practice Exercise

Take a historical narrative you like and write three predictions that would be false if the
mechanism were wrong.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
