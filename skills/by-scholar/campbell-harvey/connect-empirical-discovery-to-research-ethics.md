# Connect empirical discovery to research ethics

## Source Scholar

Campbell Harvey

## Generalisable Skill

Treat fragile significance, p-hacking, and publication bias as methodological and ethical issues.

## Evidence Anchors

- Backtesting
- Factor zoo papers
- Research credibility writing

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests audit how research practices affect published findings.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction often explains how standard incentives can produce misleading evidence.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill when building new empirical workflows or replication standards.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is that not every fragile result is misconduct; many are uncertainty.

## Practice Exercise

Write a disclosure paragraph for your specification search.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
