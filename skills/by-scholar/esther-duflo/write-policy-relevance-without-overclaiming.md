# Write policy relevance without overclaiming

## Source Scholar

Esther Duflo

## Generalisable Skill

State why the problem matters for policy while being precise about setting, treatment, and external
validity.

## Evidence Anchors

- Randomization in Development Economics
- Microfinance studies
- Poverty program studies

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The empirical evidence is often localized; the writing makes broader relevance through mechanism and
careful interpretation.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The abstract usually tells readers the policy issue, the intervention, the design, and the key
margin learned.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by writing policy implications as conditional lessons: if the same constraint binds, this
evidence is informative.

### Critical boundary

The limitation is that policymakers may want universal answers. Tasteful research resists universal
claims from one setting.

## Practice Exercise

Rewrite a broad policy conclusion into a mechanism-based conditional statement.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
