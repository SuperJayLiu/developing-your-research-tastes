# Connect content to incentives

## Source Scholar

Matthew Gentzkow

## Generalisable Skill

Explain media or communication choices as responses to demand, competition, reputation, or strategic
incentives.

## Evidence Anchors

- What Drives Media Slant?
- Media Bias and Reputation
- Media market studies

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The empirical/theoretical tests separate supply-side ideology from demand-side incentives and market
competition.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The writing frames content not as neutral description but as an equilibrium outcome.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this in any setting where organizations produce information: analysts, firms, media,
platforms, or central banks.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is that incentives are often inferred indirectly. Good taste uses alternative
explanations and robustness.

## Practice Exercise

Choose one information producer and list demand-side and supply-side incentives shaping its message.
