# Measure informational objects directly

## Source Scholar

Matthew Gentzkow

## Generalisable Skill

Turn slant, ideology, persuasion, attention, or narratives into variables that can be validated and
linked to behavior.

## Evidence Anchors

- What Drives Media Slant?
- Text as Data
- Ideological Segregation Online and Offline

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests use text, consumption data, or market outcomes to measure information environments and
then connect them to incentives.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction usually begins with an important but vague informational concept and promises a way
to measure it.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill when your mechanism is informational but your data only indirectly observe
information.

### Critical boundary

The risk is measurement reification: a text score is not the concept itself. Validation is part of
the contribution.

## Practice Exercise

Pick one vague concept and propose two validation tests for a text-based measure.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
