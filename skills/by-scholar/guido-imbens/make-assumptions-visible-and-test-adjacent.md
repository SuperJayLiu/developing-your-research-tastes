# Make assumptions visible and test-adjacent

## Source Scholar

Guido Imbens

## Generalisable Skill

State identifying assumptions in a way that suggests diagnostics, sensitivity checks, or
falsification tests.

## Evidence Anchors

- LATE papers
- Matching methods
- Randomized experiment econometrics

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The formal contribution maps assumptions to observable or partially observable implications, making
causal claims more auditable.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The writing style generally favors assumption-by-assumption exposition instead of burying
identification in algebra.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by converting every identifying assumption into one diagnostic or placebo question.

### Critical boundary

Some assumptions cannot be tested directly. The skill is to make them discussable, not to pretend
they are verified.

## Practice Exercise

List three assumptions in your project and one observable pattern that would make each less
plausible.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
