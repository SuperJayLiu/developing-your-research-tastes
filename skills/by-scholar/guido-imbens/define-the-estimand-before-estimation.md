# Define the estimand before estimation

## Source Scholar

Guido Imbens

## Generalisable Skill

Specify the causal quantity of interest before choosing the estimator, covariates, or machine-
learning procedure.

## Evidence Anchors

- Identification and Estimation of Local Average Treatment Effects
- The Econometrics of Randomized Experiments
- Matching methods and treatment-effect papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The theoretical tests clarify which assumptions identify which treatment-effect parameter,
separating average effects, local effects, and conditional effects.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The writing pattern is to make the object of inference explicit early, reducing ambiguity about what
the paper claims to learn.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by writing the estimand in words and notation before looking at data.

### Critical boundary

The risk is formal clarity without substantive relevance. An estimand can be clean but not important
for the question.

## Practice Exercise

For a paper you read, write the estimand the authors actually identify and the estimand readers
might wish they had.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
