# Write finance with welfare interpretation

## Source Scholar

John Campbell

## Generalisable Skill

Translate asset-pricing or household-finance results into welfare stakes where possible.

## Evidence Anchors

- Household Finance
- Asset allocation papers
- Consumption-based asset pricing

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests ask not only whether behavior is predictable but whether it affects lifetime utility or
risk exposure.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction frames results around welfare losses, insurance, or risk-sharing.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by adding a welfare paragraph to empirical finance papers.

### Critical boundary

The risk is welfare calculations relying on strong preference assumptions.

## Practice Exercise

Write the welfare implication of one finance result and the preference assumption behind it.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
