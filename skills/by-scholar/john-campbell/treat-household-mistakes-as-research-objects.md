# Treat household mistakes as research objects

## Source Scholar

John Campbell

## Generalisable Skill

Study household financial decisions as welfare-relevant choices subject to constraints, biases, and
financial literacy.

## Evidence Anchors

- Household Finance
- Mortgage and portfolio choice papers
- Asset allocation studies

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests compare observed household choices to benchmark decisions and estimate costs of mistakes.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction makes the case that finance research should study ordinary households, not only
markets.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill for credit, insurance, retirement, housing, and portfolio decisions.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is paternalism: mistakes must be distinguished from preferences or constraints.

## Practice Exercise

Pick one household finance behavior and list benchmark, bias, and constraint explanations.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
