# Connect elasticities to welfare

## Source Scholar

Emmanuel Saez

## Generalisable Skill

Do not stop at estimating behavior; translate behavioral responses into welfare or policy
implications.

## Evidence Anchors

- Using Elasticities to Derive Optimal Income Tax Rates
- Bunching at kink points
- Tax response papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests estimate behavioral margins and then use sufficient-statistic formulas to interpret policy
tradeoffs.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction frames the empirical estimate as necessary for a policy formula, not only as a
reduced-form fact.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill when policy relevance depends on a behavioral parameter.

### Critical boundary

The risk is that sufficient statistics hide assumptions about welfare weights, avoidance, or
incidence.

## Practice Exercise

Write the policy formula your estimate would enter and list the assumptions behind it.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
