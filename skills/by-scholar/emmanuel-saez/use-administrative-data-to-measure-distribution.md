# Use administrative data to measure distribution

## Source Scholar

Emmanuel Saez

## Generalisable Skill

Use tax or administrative records to make inequality, income, wealth, and responses measurable at
the top and across the distribution.

## Evidence Anchors

- Income Inequality in the United States
- Top income shares work
- Distributional National Accounts

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The empirical tests construct consistent series and examine changes across groups and policy
regimes.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The writing emphasizes why survey or aggregate data miss key distributional facts.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by identifying which administrative record best measures the tail or hidden margin in
your topic.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is that tax data reflect both real activity and reporting responses.

## Practice Exercise

Choose one inequality measure and state what administrative data improve and distort.
