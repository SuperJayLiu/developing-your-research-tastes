# Use bunching as behavioral revealed preference

## Source Scholar

Emmanuel Saez

## Generalisable Skill

Study excess mass near tax or policy thresholds to infer behavioral responses to incentives.

## Evidence Anchors

- Do Taxpayers Bunch at Kink Points?
- Tax kink and notch studies
- Public finance bunching papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests compare observed density around thresholds to counterfactual smooth distributions.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction often motivates a policy threshold as a natural laboratory for behavior.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill anywhere rules create kinks, notches, or discontinuities: taxes, benefits, credit,
regulation.

### Critical boundary

The risk is manipulation, salience, or optimization frictions that make bunching hard to interpret.

## Practice Exercise

Find one policy threshold and write what behavior would generate bunching.
