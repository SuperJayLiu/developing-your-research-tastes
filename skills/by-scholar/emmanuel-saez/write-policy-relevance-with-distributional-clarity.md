# Write policy relevance with distributional clarity

## Source Scholar

Emmanuel Saez

## Generalisable Skill

Always state whose behavior, income, welfare, or tax burden is being measured.

## Evidence Anchors

- Top income studies
- Optimal tax papers
- Distributional accounts

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests break results by income rank, group, or policy margin, linking distribution to design.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction frames policy stakes through both efficiency and distribution.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by adding a distributional interpretation to every policy estimate.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is normative: distributional clarity does not by itself choose social weights.

## Practice Exercise

Rewrite a policy conclusion to say explicitly who gains, who loses, and what is uncertain.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
