# Privilege transparency over cleverness

## Source Scholar

David Card

## Generalisable Skill

Use designs and tables that readers can understand, replicate, and argue with rather than relying on
opaque sophistication.

## Evidence Anchors

- Minimum wage case studies
- Mariel Boatlift
- Education returns papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests often make the comparison visible before adding econometric refinements.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The writing is direct: explain the event, comparison group, outcome, and potential confounds.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by making your first empirical table the simplest version of the design.

### Critical boundary

The risk is that simple comparisons can hide unobserved differences. Transparency must be paired
with threat analysis.

## Practice Exercise

Create a one-figure version of your identification strategy.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
