# Connect market prices to real corporate actions

## Source Scholar

Jeremy Stein

## Generalisable Skill

Ask when stock market mispricing or valuation affects investment, issuance, mergers, or payout.

## Evidence Anchors

- Inefficient Investment and Stock Market Valuation
- Market Timing and Capital Structure
- Behavioral corporate finance papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests link valuation measures or sentiment to real corporate decisions.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction frames prices as potentially causal signals or incentives, not just outcomes.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this when market conditions may change firm behavior.

### Critical boundary

The risk is reverse causality: prices may predict decisions because they reflect fundamentals.

## Practice Exercise

Write a design that separates mispricing from fundamentals in one corporate decision.
