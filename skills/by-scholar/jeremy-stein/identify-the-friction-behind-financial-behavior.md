# Identify the friction behind financial behavior

## Source Scholar

Jeremy Stein

## Generalisable Skill

Do not describe financing or investment patterns only as correlations; name the friction that makes
the behavior rational or predictable.

## Evidence Anchors

- Market Timing and Capital Structure
- Inefficient Investment and Stock Market Valuation
- Behavioral asset pricing work

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The theory and tests distinguish market timing, agency, mispricing, constraints, and managerial
incentives.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction typically presents a puzzling financial behavior and then isolates the friction.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill in corporate finance, asset pricing, banking, and macro-finance.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is that many frictions imply similar behavior. Tests must separate mechanisms.

## Practice Exercise

Pick one corporate action and list three frictions that could explain it.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
