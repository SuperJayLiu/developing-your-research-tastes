# Write simple theory for empirical finance

## Source Scholar

Jeremy Stein

## Generalisable Skill

Use small models to make empirical predictions sharp enough to test.

## Evidence Anchors

- Behavioral Approach to Asset Pricing
- Corporate finance theory papers
- Financial stability work

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The theory produces predictions about cross-sectional or time-series behavior under specific
frictions.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction makes clear what empirical pattern the model organizes.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill by writing the minimal model that distinguishes your mechanism from alternatives.

### Critical boundary

The risk is toy models that are too flexible. Good taste uses the model to rule things out.

## Practice Exercise

Write one comparative static that would fail under a rival theory.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
