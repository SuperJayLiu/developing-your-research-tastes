# Use portfolios to reveal systematic patterns

## Source Scholar

Eugene Fama

## Generalisable Skill

Sort assets into portfolios to make return patterns visible, robust, and economically interpretable.

## Evidence Anchors

- The Cross-Section of Expected Stock Returns
- Common Risk Factors
- Factor-model papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests compare average returns across size, value, market, or other characteristic-sorted
portfolios.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction frames characteristics as candidates for systematic risk or pricing errors.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by constructing portfolios that express the mechanism rather than only maximizing
t-statistics.

### Critical boundary

The risk is data mining. Good taste requires out-of-sample logic, economic motivation, and
robustness.

## Practice Exercise

Design a portfolio sort for one hypothesized return mechanism and state the predicted pattern.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
