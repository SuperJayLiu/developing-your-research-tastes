# Connect behavioral finance to real decisions

## Source Scholar

Ulrike Malmendier

## Generalisable Skill

Show how beliefs and biases affect investment, financing, labor, or household outcomes, not just
prices.

## Evidence Anchors

- CEO Overconfidence
- Experience effects
- Household finance studies

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests link psychological variables to real corporate or household decisions.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction makes behavioral finance economically consequential by identifying the decision
margin.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill when a behavioral mechanism needs real effects.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is effect size and selection. Behavioral stories must show economic magnitude.

## Practice Exercise

Choose one bias and write the real decision it would change.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
