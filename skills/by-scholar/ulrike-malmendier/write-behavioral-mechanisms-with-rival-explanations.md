# Write behavioral mechanisms with rival explanations

## Source Scholar

Ulrike Malmendier

## Generalisable Skill

Make behavioral claims credible by stating and testing rational or institutional alternatives.

## Evidence Anchors

- CEO Overconfidence
- Experience effects
- Behavioral finance papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests often compare behavioral proxies against controls for incentives, information, and
constraints.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The writing anticipates skepticism and builds the case through exclusion of alternatives.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by adding a 'why not rational learning?' section.

### Critical boundary

The risk is labeling unexplained residuals as psychology.

## Practice Exercise

Write three rational alternatives to your behavioral mechanism.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
