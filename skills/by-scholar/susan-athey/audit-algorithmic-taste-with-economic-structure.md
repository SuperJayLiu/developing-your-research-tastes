# Audit algorithmic taste with economic structure

## Source Scholar

Susan Athey

## Generalisable Skill

Ask whether an algorithmic recommendation respects incentives, equilibrium, fairness, and policy
constraints.

## Evidence Anchors

- Policy learning papers
- Platform design papers
- ML for economics writing

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests go beyond fit to consider treatment assignment, decision rules, welfare criteria, and
unintended behavior.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction often emphasizes that better decisions require both data and economic objectives.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill in AI-for-research projects: every AI suggestion should be checked against the
economic object and decision context.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is that some objectives are normative. The paper should state whose welfare or loss
function is optimized.

## Practice Exercise

Write the objective function behind one algorithmic recommendation you use.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
