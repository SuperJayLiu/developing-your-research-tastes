# Connect econometrics to economic primitives

## Source Scholar

Lars Peter Hansen

## Generalisable Skill

Make statistical restrictions interpretable as preferences, technologies, constraints, or
information.

## Evidence Anchors

- Asset pricing tests
- GMM applications
- Macroeconomic uncertainty work

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests map moments to economic mechanisms rather than treating them as purely statistical.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction explains why the restriction matters economically.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill by labeling each empirical restriction with the primitive that generates it.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is weak identification of primitives.

## Practice Exercise

Take one moment condition and write the economic behavior behind it.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
