# Treat model misspecification as normal

## Source Scholar

Lars Peter Hansen

## Generalisable Skill

Analyze how conclusions change when the model is approximate rather than exactly true.

## Evidence Anchors

- Robustness papers
- Uncertainty work
- Asset pricing econometrics

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests evaluate sensitivity to perturbations, alternative specifications, and model uncertainty.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction often rejects false certainty and asks what survives ambiguity.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by adding a model-uncertainty section to structural or asset-pricing work.

### Critical boundary

The risk is paralysis: if every model is wrong, the paper still needs a decision criterion.

## Practice Exercise

State what result must survive misspecification to be useful.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
