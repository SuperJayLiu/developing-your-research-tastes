# Discipline models with moment restrictions

## Source Scholar

Lars Peter Hansen

## Generalisable Skill

Translate theory into testable moment conditions that data can reject.

## Evidence Anchors

- GMM paper
- Asset pricing GMM applications
- Econometric method work

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests ask whether theoretical restrictions match sample moments and whether overidentifying
restrictions hold.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction frames the method as a bridge between structural theory and empirical scrutiny.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill when a model implies restrictions but full likelihood is not credible.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is that passing moments does not prove the model; failing moments may reflect
measurement.

## Practice Exercise

Write three moment restrictions implied by one theory.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
