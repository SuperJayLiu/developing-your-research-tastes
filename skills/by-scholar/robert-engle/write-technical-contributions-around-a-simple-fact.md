# Write technical contributions around a simple fact

## Source Scholar

Robert Engle

## Generalisable Skill

Anchor a technical paper in a plain empirical regularity readers immediately recognize.

## Evidence Anchors

- ARCH paper
- DCC paper
- Volatility model papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The empirical fact—volatility clusters, correlations move—is made visible before the model is
formalized.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction makes the reader feel the inadequacy of existing tools.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by opening technical work with the simplest figure or fact motivating the method.

### Critical boundary

The risk is overfitting a model to one stylized fact while ignoring economic meaning.

## Practice Exercise

Find the one graph that makes your methods paper necessary.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
