# Turn crisis narratives into state-variable models

## Source Scholar

Markus Brunnermeier

## Generalisable Skill

Translate crisis stories into dynamic models with leverage, constraints, beliefs, or liquidity as
state variables.

## Evidence Anchors

- Liquidity and credit crunch analysis
- I Theory of Money
- Systemic risk papers

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests and models identify variables that propagate shocks and predict fragility.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction converts an episode into a general mechanism.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill when historical crisis accounts are rich but analytically loose.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is overfitting one crisis. The mechanism should travel across episodes.

## Practice Exercise

Choose one crisis story and name the state variable that moves through it.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
