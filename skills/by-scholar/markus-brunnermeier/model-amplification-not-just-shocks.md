# Model amplification, not just shocks

## Source Scholar

Markus Brunnermeier

## Generalisable Skill

Ask how a small shock becomes a crisis through leverage, liquidity, margins, and feedback.

## Evidence Anchors

- Market Liquidity and Funding Liquidity
- Deciphering the Liquidity and Credit Crunch
- Bubbles and Crashes

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The theory and evidence trace feedback loops between funding constraints, asset sales, prices, and
balance sheets.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction often starts with an observed crisis pattern and asks why the response is
nonlinear.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill in banking, asset markets, macro shocks, and supply chains.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is that amplification mechanisms need timing and state variables, not only narrative
arrows.

## Practice Exercise

Draw a feedback loop for one crisis mechanism and mark the state variable.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
