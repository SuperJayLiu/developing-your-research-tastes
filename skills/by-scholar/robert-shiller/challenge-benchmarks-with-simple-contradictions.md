# Challenge benchmarks with simple contradictions

## Source Scholar

Robert Shiller

## Generalisable Skill

Use a simple empirical contradiction to force reconsideration of a dominant model.

## Evidence Anchors

- Do Stock Prices Move Too Much
- Long-run valuation studies
- Survey expectation work

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests are often conceptually simple even if technically debated: compare model-implied variation
to observed variation.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction makes the reader see why the contradiction matters for the whole framework.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill when a large theory produces a clear implication that data visibly violate.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is that simple contradictions can be resolved by adding risks, frictions, or
expectations heterogeneity.

## Practice Exercise

Find one benchmark implication and one fact that would make it uncomfortable.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
