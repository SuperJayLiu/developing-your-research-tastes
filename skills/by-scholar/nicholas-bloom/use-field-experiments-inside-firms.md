# Use field experiments inside firms

## Source Scholar

Nicholas Bloom

## Generalisable Skill

Test management and productivity mechanisms by intervening within organizations rather than only
observing across them.

## Evidence Anchors

- Does Management Matter?
- Management intervention studies
- Working-from-home studies

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The empirical tests use randomized or quasi-random changes in practices to estimate productivity and
behavior effects.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The writing emphasizes why the firm interior matters and how access permits a cleaner test.

### Why this skill travels

Apply this by asking whether a firm policy can be randomized, phased in, or compared across units.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is external validity: one firm or country may not represent all organizations.

## Practice Exercise

Write one within-organization experiment that could test a productivity mechanism.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
