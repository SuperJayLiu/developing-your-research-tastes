# Make hidden organizational practices measurable

## Source Scholar

Nicholas Bloom

## Generalisable Skill

Convert management, organization, and workplace practices into comparable measures that can explain
productivity variation.

## Evidence Anchors

- Measuring and Explaining Management Practices
- Does Management Matter?
- Firm organization studies

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests use surveys, experiments, and cross-firm comparisons to link management practices to
outcomes.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction frames an important residual—productivity differences—as partly explainable by a
hard-to-measure organizational variable.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill whenever the likely mechanism is inside the firm or institution rather than in prices
alone.

### Critical boundary

The risk is survey measurement error and reverse causality. Good taste validates measures and seeks
exogenous variation.

## Practice Exercise

Design three survey questions for a hidden organizational practice and one validation check.

## How to Use This Skill in Your Own Research

1. Restate your research idea using this skill.
2. Name the theory or mechanism it implies.
3. State one empirical or theoretical test that could reject it.
4. Identify one rival explanation.
5. Write the introduction paragraph that would make the reader care.
