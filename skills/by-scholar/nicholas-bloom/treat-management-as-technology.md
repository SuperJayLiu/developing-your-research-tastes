# Treat management as technology

## Source Scholar

Nicholas Bloom

## Generalisable Skill

Analyze management practices as productive technologies that diffuse, vary in quality, and interact
with competition and skills.

## Evidence Anchors

- Management practices across countries
- Does Management Matter?
- Firm productivity studies

## Taste Extraction Notes

### What the papers tend to test

The tests look for variation in adoption, quality, diffusion, and effects on productivity.

### What the abstract/introduction tends to do

The introduction turns a soft concept into an economic input with measurable returns.

### Why this skill travels

Use this skill in finance, public economics, education, or health organizations where internal
practices matter.

### Critical boundary

The danger is assuming best practices are universally optimal. Fit with local constraints matters.

## Practice Exercise

Choose one management practice and state when it would have high and low returns.
