# Derive Testable Predictions

## One-Sentence Skill

Turn a theory into predictions that can be contradicted by data.

## What the Skill Means

This skill should be developed with concrete paper evidence, scholar examples, and practice exercises.

## Why It Matters

It helps prevent a common research weakness: having a method, dataset, or result without a clear research judgment behind it.

## Good Taste

- The skill improves the paper's core argument.
- The skill clarifies what would convince a skeptical reader.
- The skill is portable to another setting.

## Bad Taste

- The skill is used mechanically.
- The paper borrows the appearance of rigor without improving judgment.
- The claim becomes stronger than the evidence.
