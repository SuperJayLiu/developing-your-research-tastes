# Theory Development Lab

Use this page to turn a skill into a theory.

## Step 1: Start with a puzzle

What fact, anomaly, pattern, or institutional detail needs explaining?

## Step 2: Name the friction

Examples:

- information asymmetry,
- agency conflict,
- liquidity constraint,
- search friction,
- behavioral bias,
- market power,
- coordination failure,
- political constraint.

## Step 3: Identify the agents

Who acts? What do they know? What do they want? What constrains them?

## Step 4: State the mechanism

Write the mechanism in one sentence.

```text
Because [friction], when [shock/change/rule] happens, [agent] changes [behavior], leading to [outcome].
```

## Step 5: Derive predictions

A useful theory should generate predictions that could be wrong.

Good predictions are:

- directional,
- observable,
- different from alternative mechanisms,
- economically meaningful.

## Step 6: Identify the hardest test

Ask:

```text
What evidence would make a skeptical reader update?
```
