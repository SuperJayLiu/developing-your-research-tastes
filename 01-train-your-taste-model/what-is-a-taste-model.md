# What Is a Taste Model?

A taste model is a researcher's internal prediction system.

It predicts whether a research idea is likely to become a good paper.

A strong taste model can judge:

- importance: does the question matter?
- novelty: does the paper change what we know?
- mechanism: is there a clear causal or economic channel?
- evidence: would the design convince a skeptical reader?
- portability: can the idea teach something beyond one setting?
- communication: can the contribution be explained clearly?

A weak taste model overweights surface features:

- a trendy topic,
- a complicated method,
- a large dataset,
- many robustness checks,
- polished writing,
- a result that sounds surprising but does not teach much.

The purpose of this repo is to help you train the model deliberately.
