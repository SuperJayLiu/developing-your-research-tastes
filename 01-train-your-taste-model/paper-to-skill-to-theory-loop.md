# Paper → Skill → Theory Loop

Use this loop whenever you read a paper.

## 1. Paper

What did the paper ask, do, and find?

## 2. Research Move

What did the authors do that was intellectually powerful?

Examples:

- found institutional variation,
- built a clean measure,
- separated competing mechanisms,
- turned a historical fact into a modern test,
- used prices to infer beliefs,
- derived a prediction that could be wrong.

## 3. Skill

Rewrite the research move as something another researcher can practice.

Example:

```text
Research move: use index inclusion rules to identify price pressure.
Skill: find identification inside market institutions.
```

## 4. Theory

Use the skill to build a theory in a new setting.

Ask:

- What is the friction?
- Who are the agents?
- What changes their incentives?
- What prediction follows?
- What would falsify the theory?

## 5. Test

Design a test that distinguishes your theory from alternatives.

## 6. Update

Write one sentence:

```text
This changed my taste because...
```
