# Joseph Stiglitz

Joseph Stiglitz is read here as a source of research judgment in economics, not as a topic list. The question is what a researcher can learn from the repeated shape of the work: what kinds of puzzles become important, what mechanisms or designs carry the argument, what evidence is treated as persuasive, and how the paper keeps its contribution within honest boundaries.

## Reading The Taste

When reading Joseph Stiglitz, focus first on the opening move. Ask how the paper convinces the reader that the question matters. Then look for the engine of the paper: the model, design, data construction, comparison, historical fact, market friction, institutional detail, or mechanism that does the real work. Finally, study the boundary. The strongest papers usually know what they have not proved.
