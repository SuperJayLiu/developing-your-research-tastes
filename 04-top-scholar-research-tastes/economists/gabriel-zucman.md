# Gabriel Zucman

## One-Sentence Research Taste

Hidden economic activity can be measured by exploiting accounting identities, cross-border
inconsistencies, and distributional data integration.

## Skill 1: Measure hidden activity through accounting gaps

### Generalisable research taste

Use inconsistencies across balance sheets, national accounts, or tax records to infer hidden wealth,
profits, or ownership.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- The Missing Wealth of Nations
- Who Owns the Wealth in Tax Havens?
- Offshore wealth studies

**What the papers tend to test**

The empirical tests compare asset and liability records, cross-border positions, and ownership data
to reveal missing stocks.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction frames the missing object as large and policy-relevant, then explains the
accounting identity that makes it observable indirectly.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Use this skill for hidden finance, informal activity, tax evasion, or unreported risks.

### Critical boundary

The danger is that accounting gaps may reflect measurement error rather than hidden behavior.

### Practice exercise

Find one accounting identity in your field and ask what residual could reveal hidden activity.

## Skill 2: Combine macro accounts with micro distribution

### Generalisable research taste

Integrate national aggregates with microdata to study who owns income or wealth, not only how large
the aggregate is.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Distributional National Accounts
- Global Wealth Inequality
- Top wealth studies

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests reconcile totals with distributional records and check sensitivity to allocation
assumptions.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The writing argues that macro facts without distribution miss the main economic question.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Apply this by asking how an aggregate change is distributed across groups or balance sheets.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is imputation: distributional allocation rules must be transparent.

### Practice exercise

Take one aggregate statistic and sketch how to distribute it across households or firms.

## Skill 3: Turn data gaps into research questions

### Generalisable research taste

Treat missing, inconsistent, or strategically hidden data as evidence of the phenomenon rather than
only as a nuisance.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Offshore wealth studies
- Tax evasion studies
- Distributional accounts

**What the papers tend to test**

The empirical tests study where official data fail and what economic behavior produces that failure.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction often starts with the fact that standard data cannot see the object of interest.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Use this skill when actors have incentives to hide behavior from official measurement.

### Critical boundary

The risk is speculative inference. A good paper triangulates multiple data sources.

### Practice exercise

List three independent traces of a hidden activity you want to study.

## Skill 4: Write measurement work as policy-relevant detective work

### Generalisable research taste

Make complex data reconciliation readable by tying every measurement choice to the policy object.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- The Missing Wealth of Nations
- Distributional National Accounts
- Tax haven studies

**What the papers tend to test**

The evidence is technical, but the introduction keeps the reader focused on why the hidden stock
changes policy conclusions.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The abstract usually emphasizes the new estimate, its magnitude, and why prior data missed it.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Apply this by opening a measurement paper with the decision error caused by bad measurement.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is rhetorical overconfidence. Detective-style writing must not outrun uncertainty
bands.

### Practice exercise

Write a one-paragraph introduction where a measurement error leads to a policy error.

## What Not to Overgeneralize

- Do not copy the scholar's topic and call it taste.
- Do not copy the method without the identification, mechanism, or modeling discipline that made it persuasive.
- Do not treat a famous paper as a universal recipe. Each skill has scope conditions.
- Do not infer too much from abstracts alone; the full introduction, design, tests, and limitations matter.
