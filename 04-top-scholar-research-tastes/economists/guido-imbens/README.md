## Reading The Taste

When reading Guido Imbens, focus first on the opening move. Ask how the paper convinces the reader that the question matters. Then look for the engine of the paper: the model, design, data construction, comparison, historical fact, market friction, institutional detail, or mechanism that does the real work. Finally, study the boundary. The strongest papers usually know what they have not proved.
