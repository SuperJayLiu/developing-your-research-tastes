## Skill 1: Turn lived experience into a state variable

### Generalisable research taste

Model past experiences as persistent variables that shape beliefs, risk-taking, and financial
decisions.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Depression Babies
- Experience Effects in Finance
- Household finance studies

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests link cohort or personal exposure to later investment, borrowing, or risk choices.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction frames experience as a mechanism that standard rational models may omit.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Use this skill in macro, finance, entrepreneurship, labor, or political economy where histories
shape beliefs.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is cohort confounding. Experience effects require careful separation from age, period,
and selection.

### Practice exercise

Pick one generation-defining event and predict a later economic behavior it might affect.

## Skill 2: Measure bias through revealed decisions

### Generalisable research taste

Infer overconfidence or bias from systematic decision patterns rather than only surveys or labels.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- CEO Overconfidence and Corporate Investment
- Behavioral corporate finance papers
- Household finance work

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests use option exercise, investment, financing, or portfolio choices as behavioral signals.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction motivates the psychological mechanism and then explains why the chosen behavior
reveals it.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Apply this by finding a costly action that reveals beliefs or bias.

### Critical boundary

The risk is observational equivalence: a behavior may reflect incentives or private information, not
bias.

### Practice exercise

List two non-bias explanations for a behavioral measure.

## Skill 3: Connect behavioral finance to real decisions

### Generalisable research taste

Show how beliefs and biases affect investment, financing, labor, or household outcomes, not just
prices.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- CEO Overconfidence
- Experience effects
- Household finance studies

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests link psychological variables to real corporate or household decisions.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction makes behavioral finance economically consequential by identifying the decision
margin.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Use this skill when a behavioral mechanism needs real effects.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is effect size and selection. Behavioral stories must show economic magnitude.

### Practice exercise

Choose one bias and write the real decision it would change.

## Skill 4: Write behavioral mechanisms with rival explanations

### Generalisable research taste

Make behavioral claims credible by stating and testing rational or institutional alternatives.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- CEO Overconfidence
- Experience effects
- Behavioral finance papers

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests often compare behavioral proxies against controls for incentives, information, and
constraints.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The writing anticipates skepticism and builds the case through exclusion of alternatives.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Apply this by adding a 'why not rational learning?' section.

### Critical boundary

The risk is labeling unexplained residuals as psychology.

### Practice exercise

Write three rational alternatives to your behavioral mechanism.
