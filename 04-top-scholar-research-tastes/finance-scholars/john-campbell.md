# John Campbell

## One-Sentence Research Taste

Asset prices and household decisions should be decomposed into interpretable risks, horizons, and
welfare-relevant mistakes.

## Extraction Lens

The skills below are extracted by asking four questions across the scholar's papers:

1. **Question taste**: What kinds of questions does the scholar repeatedly choose?
2. **Design or theory taste**: What empirical design, model structure, or mechanism makes the question answerable?
3. **Testing taste**: What would the paper have to show for the claim to be believable?
4. **Writing taste**: How do the abstract and introduction make the contribution legible?

The goal is not to imitate the scholar's topic. The goal is to extract a transferable judgment rule.

## Signature Questions

- What hidden mechanism makes a large economic or financial fact understandable?
- What design, model, or measurement choice turns that mechanism into evidence?
- What alternative explanation must be ruled out before the claim becomes credible?

## Skill 1: Decompose return variation into economic news

### Generalisable research taste

Separate movements in returns into cash-flow news, discount-rate news, and changing risk
perceptions.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- A Variance Decomposition for Stock Returns
- Bad Beta, Good Beta
- Return predictability work

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests use VARs and present-value restrictions to separate components of return variation.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction frames asset pricing as a question of what news prices are reacting to.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Use this skill in asset pricing, macro-finance, and valuation.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is model dependence in decompositions and horizons.

### Practice exercise

Take one return movement and write three news interpretations.

## Skill 2: Connect horizon to risk

### Generalisable research taste

Ask how the relevant investment horizon changes risk exposure and optimal decisions.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Long-run risks and habit-related work
- Asset allocation papers
- Household finance work

**What the papers tend to test**

The theory and tests examine how risks vary across horizons and investor types.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction frames average risk as incomplete without horizon.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Apply this in pensions, household finance, climate risk, and corporate investment.

### Critical boundary

The risk is assuming investors share the same horizon or constraints.

### Practice exercise

Write how a one-year and thirty-year investor see the same asset differently.

## Skill 3: Treat household mistakes as research objects

### Generalisable research taste

Study household financial decisions as welfare-relevant choices subject to constraints, biases, and
financial literacy.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Household Finance
- Mortgage and portfolio choice papers
- Asset allocation studies

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests compare observed household choices to benchmark decisions and estimate costs of mistakes.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction makes the case that finance research should study ordinary households, not only
markets.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Use this skill for credit, insurance, retirement, housing, and portfolio decisions.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is paternalism: mistakes must be distinguished from preferences or constraints.

### Practice exercise

Pick one household finance behavior and list benchmark, bias, and constraint explanations.

## Skill 4: Write finance with welfare interpretation

### Generalisable research taste

Translate asset-pricing or household-finance results into welfare stakes where possible.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Household Finance
- Asset allocation papers
- Consumption-based asset pricing

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests ask not only whether behavior is predictable but whether it affects lifetime utility or
risk exposure.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction frames results around welfare losses, insurance, or risk-sharing.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Apply this by adding a welfare paragraph to empirical finance papers.

### Critical boundary

The risk is welfare calculations relying on strong preference assumptions.

### Practice exercise

Write the welfare implication of one finance result and the preference assumption behind it.

## What Not to Overgeneralize

- Do not copy the scholar's topic and call it taste.
- Do not copy the method without the identification, mechanism, or modeling discipline that made it persuasive.
- Do not treat a famous paper as a universal recipe. Each skill has scope conditions.
- Do not infer too much from abstracts alone; the full introduction, design, tests, and limitations matter.
