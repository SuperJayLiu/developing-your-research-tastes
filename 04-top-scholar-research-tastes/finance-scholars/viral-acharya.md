# Viral Acharya

## One-Sentence Research Taste

Financial institutions should be studied as interconnected balance sheets whose private risk choices
can create system-wide externalities.

## Extraction Lens

The skills below are extracted by asking four questions across the scholar's papers:

1. **Question taste**: What kinds of questions does the scholar repeatedly choose?
2. **Design or theory taste**: What empirical design, model structure, or mechanism makes the question answerable?
3. **Testing taste**: What would the paper have to show for the claim to be believable?
4. **Writing taste**: How do the abstract and introduction make the contribution legible?

The goal is not to imitate the scholar's topic. The goal is to extract a transferable judgment rule.

## Skill 1: Measure systemic risk as marginal contribution

### Generalisable research taste

Ask how much an institution contributes to system-wide losses in distress, not only how risky it is
alone.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Measuring Systemic Risk
- SRISK-related work
- Bank systemic risk studies

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests estimate downside exposure, capitalization shortfalls, and correlations with market
distress.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction reframes regulation around externality contribution rather than individual failure
probability.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Use this skill in banking, asset management, insurance, or climate finance.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is model risk in tail estimation and market-based measures.

### Practice exercise

For one institution, distinguish standalone risk from system contribution.

## Skill 2: Connect bank balance sheets to macro outcomes

### Generalisable research taste

Trace how bank capital, liquidity, and sovereign exposure affect credit supply and real activity.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Banking crisis papers
- Sovereign debt and banks
- Credit supply studies

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests link bank-level balance-sheet shocks to lending, firms, and macro outcomes.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction frames banks as transmission mechanisms, not passive intermediaries.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Apply this by identifying the balance-sheet item that transmits a shock.

### Critical boundary

The risk is simultaneity between bank health and borrower demand.

### Practice exercise

Write a bank-shock identification strategy with one demand control.

## Skill 3: Design regulation around externalities

### Generalisable research taste

Evaluate prudential rules by the risk externalities they reduce, not only by private bank
incentives.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Systemic risk regulation theory
- Measuring Systemic Risk
- Macroprudential papers

**What the papers tend to test**

The theory tests compare private incentives with social costs under distress.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction makes the normative object explicit: why private risk choices are socially
inefficient.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Use this skill whenever studying regulation of interconnected agents.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is implementation: measuring externalities accurately enough for policy is hard.

### Practice exercise

List the private benefit and social cost of one risky financial activity.

## Skill 4: Write crisis papers with balance-sheet mechanics

### Generalisable research taste

Explain crises through concrete balance-sheet channels rather than broad panic language.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Seeds of a Crisis
- Systemic risk papers
- Sovereign-bank nexus work

**What the papers tend to test**

The evidence shows which assets, liabilities, or funding structures create fragility.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction makes the crisis mechanism visible through institutional detail.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Apply this by replacing abstract crisis terms with balance-sheet entries.

### Critical boundary

The risk is missing expectations and strategic behavior beyond accounting.

### Practice exercise

Write a crisis mechanism using assets, liabilities, capital, and funding.

## What Not to Overgeneralize
