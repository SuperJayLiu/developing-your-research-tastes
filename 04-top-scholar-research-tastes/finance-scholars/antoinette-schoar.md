# Antoinette Schoar

## One-Sentence Research Taste

Financial behavior is heterogeneous because households, entrepreneurs, and managers face different
information, trust, incentives, and constraints.

## Extraction Lens

The skills below are extracted by asking four questions across the scholar's papers:

1. **Question taste**: What kinds of questions does the scholar repeatedly choose?
2. **Design or theory taste**: What empirical design, model structure, or mechanism makes the question answerable?
3. **Testing taste**: What would the paper have to show for the claim to be believable?
4. **Writing taste**: How do the abstract and introduction make the contribution legible?

The goal is not to imitate the scholar's topic. The goal is to extract a transferable judgment rule.

## Skill 1: Study financial decisions at the person level

### Generalisable research taste

Use microdata to understand heterogeneity in household, entrepreneur, or manager decisions.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Household finance papers
- Entrepreneurial finance studies
- Consumer finance work

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests link individual characteristics, advice, constraints, or experience to financial choices.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction frames aggregate outcomes as emerging from heterogeneous decision-makers.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Use this skill when average effects hide who responds and why.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is privacy, selection, and unobserved preferences.

### Practice exercise

Write the individual-level variable you need to explain one aggregate finance fact.

## Skill 2: Treat financial intermediation as advice and trust

### Generalisable research taste

Study intermediaries not only as capital providers but as information and trust channels.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Financial advice studies
- Household finance work
- Consumer finance papers

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests examine advisor incentives, consumer mistakes, trust, and product choice.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction reframes intermediation around information asymmetry and behavioral frictions.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Apply this in banking, wealth management, fintech, and insurance.

### Critical boundary

The risk is blaming consumers while ignoring product complexity or incentives.

### Practice exercise

Map the advice chain in one financial product.

## Skill 3: Connect entrepreneurial finance to real capabilities

### Generalisable research taste

Explain entrepreneurial outcomes through financing, management, networks, and founder traits
jointly.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Entrepreneurial finance studies
- Management and CEO work
- Firm microdata papers

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests link financing access to investment, growth, survival, and managerial decisions.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction frames finance as enabling or constraining real capability.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Use this skill when studying startups, small firms, or family businesses.

### Critical boundary

The boundary is separating financing constraints from entrepreneur quality.

### Practice exercise

Write two tests that distinguish credit constraints from low demand.

## Skill 4: Write heterogeneity as the main result

### Generalisable research taste

Make variation across people or firms central rather than relegating it to robustness tables.

### How this taste is extracted

**Evidence anchors**

- Household finance studies
- Entrepreneurial finance work
- Consumer finance papers

**What the papers tend to test**

The tests estimate who benefits, who is harmed, and which constraints bind for each group.

**What the abstract/introduction tends to do**

The introduction motivates why average effects are insufficient.

### Why this becomes a transferable skill

Apply this by naming the heterogeneity dimension in the research question.

### Critical boundary

The risk is too many cuts of the data. Heterogeneity needs theory.

### Practice exercise

Choose one heterogeneity dimension and justify it before seeing results.

## What Not to Overgeneralize

- Do not copy the scholar's topic and call it taste.
