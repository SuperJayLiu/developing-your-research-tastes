# Gotchas For Nobel-Derived Research Taste

The gotchas are the highest-signal part of this skill. Add to this file whenever a generated skill card sounds smart but fails to change a research decision.

## Topic Is Not Taste

Do not write "study institutions," "study poverty," "study asset prices," or "study innovation" as a skill. Those are topic labels. A skill must say what move to perform. A stronger version is "translate an institution into a changed constraint and identify the comparison that reveals the constraint." Another stronger version is "separate risk, beliefs, liquidity, and mispricing before interpreting a price pattern."

## Fame Is Not Evidence

Do not infer a skill from the fact that a scholar won a prize. Use prize lectures, scientific background documents, scholar pages, canonical papers, or other evidence. The Nobel label can help organize the map, but it cannot substitute for reading the work.

## Big Question, Thin Evidence

Top scholars often ask large questions, but they usually pair those questions with disciplined evidence, theory, or boundary conditions. A bad imitation copies only the scale. The warning sign is a card that encourages "ask bigger questions" without explaining what design, model, comparison, or measurement can carry the claim.

## Method As Contribution

A method is not automatically a research contribution. The card should explain what the method lets the researcher learn that was previously hard to learn. If the skill says "use IV," "use RCTs," "build a structural model," or "estimate a factor model" without naming the learning object and threat, it is immature.

## Mechanism After Result

Do not add mechanism language after a result has already been interpreted. A mature skill asks what evidence would distinguish mechanisms before the result is used rhetorically. If the card only says "then test mechanisms," it should be revised to say which competing mechanisms would otherwise explain the same pattern.

## Institutional Scenery

Institutional detail is useful only if it changes constraints, incentives, information, enforcement, bargaining, or feasible actions. A long paragraph of legal or historical context is not institutional taste unless the rule becomes a testable mechanism.

## Price Pattern As Explanation

Prices and returns aggregate many forces. A card that treats predictability, volatility, spreads, or crisis movements as self-explanatory is weak. The mature version names the benchmark and candidate interpretations before assigning a mechanism.

## Welfare Without A Welfare Object

Do not let a card claim welfare relevance because the topic is socially important. State the welfare object, the affected agents, the policy margin, and the assumptions required to move from effect to welfare. If those are absent, the card should use narrower language.

## Elegant Model, No Load-Bearing Assumption

A theory card is weak if the model's assumptions do not change the prediction or welfare comparison. The mature version asks what happens in the benchmark and which single departure carries the result.

## Robustness Without Learning

Adding checks is not the same as learning. A robustness check should target a specific threat. If the card says "run robustness checks" without naming selection, measurement error, timing, anticipation, omitted shocks, equilibrium feedback, or extrapolation, the instruction is too shallow.

## Scholar Imitation Without Transfer

A skill is not mature until it travels to a different project. The transfer test is simple: after applying the card, what research decision changes? If the answer is "I admire the scholar more," the card is still a note.
