# What Is Research Taste?

Research taste is the judgment behind good research choices.

It is the ability to sense:

- which questions are important rather than merely clever,
- which mechanisms are plausible rather than decorative,
- which empirical designs are credible rather than convenient,
- which measurements capture the concept rather than the noise,
- which results change what we believe,
- which papers have a real contribution.

In economics and finance, research taste is especially important because many projects can be technically correct but intellectually weak.

A project may have clean code, strong econometrics, and polished writing, yet still fail because the question is small, the mechanism is vague, the evidence is not decisive, or the contribution is not clear.

This repo treats research taste as a trainable model.

You improve it by repeatedly doing this:

```text
Observe great research → extract the research move → turn it into a skill → practice the skill → update your judgment
```
